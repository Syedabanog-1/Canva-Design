# Voltage Grid

**Movement Name:** Voltage Grid

## Space and Form

The composition is structured on a disciplined vertical rhythm — a deep LinkedIn-blue header commanding the upper third, a luminous near-white content field below, and a thin electric accent bar anchoring the base. Every element is placed on an invisible 90px grid, creating the mathematical precision of a circuit board. White space is treated as a structural material, not emptiness — generous margins enforce clarity, while tightly spaced type clusters communicate density of thought without visual noise.

## Color and Signal

LinkedIn Blue (#0A66C2) is the primary carrier of authority. It saturates the header gradient from a near-navy (#053D75) down to the brand blue, signaling professionalism and platform context simultaneously. Deep Purple (#4A154B) and Teal (#0F6E56) serve as secondary signal colors — each anchoring one badge with its own frequency. The background remains surgical white (#F8F9FA), letting the colored accent bars on each card act as visual bookmarks the eye can scan at thumbnail resolution.

## Typography

BigShoulders Bold carries the headline — condensed, commanding, technical. It compresses maximum meaning into minimum horizontal space, a metaphor for the 10-80-10 rule itself. WorkSans Bold drives card titles — geometric, modern, screen-optimized. Jura Medium labels the tags — its monospaced soul signals machine-readable precision. InstrumentSans anchors the footer — balanced weight between the boldness above and the intimacy of a credit line. Every type decision reinforces the thesis: structured intelligence over decorative flair.

## Craftsmanship

This graphic is meticulously crafted — every pixel placed with intention, every spacing decision traced back to the grid, every color value chosen for signal rather than decoration. The drop shadows on cards use four layers of decreasing opacity to simulate material depth without kitsch. The node circles straddling the header-body boundary were painstakingly sized so their 50% overlap creates a continuous bridge between zones. The hex watermark traces in the header corners echo circuit topography. This is the product of deep expertise and countless refinements — the kind of work that looks effortless precisely because every effort has been concealed within its precision.

## Restraint

The total word count on the graphic is held under 60. Each card communicates one idea in three levels of zoom: tag (at-a-glance), title (3-second read), detail (engaged reader). No stock photography — only geometric forms, node circles, and accent bars. The design must survive compression to a 150×150px thumbnail: the headline must still hit, the three color-coded cards must still read as three distinct sections, the footer must still ground the piece with authorship. Restraint is not limitation — it is the discipline that makes the work trustworthy.
